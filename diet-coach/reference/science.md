# Evidence base

The recommendations in this skill are grounded in the following research. Cite the
mechanism in plain language to the user; link sources only if asked.

## Energy & metabolism

- **Mifflin-St Jeor** is the most accurate general BMR equation — a 2005
  systematic review found it predicted resting metabolic rate within 10% of
  measured values more often than other equations. Activity multipliers 1.2–1.9
  estimate TDEE.
- A deficit of **500–750 kcal/day** produces roughly **0.5–1 kg/week** of loss.
  Target a rate of **0.5–1% of body weight per week**; faster loss increases
  muscle loss and rebound risk.

## Protein & muscle retention

- During an energy deficit, **higher protein (≈1.8–2.7 g/kg body weight, up to
  ~3 g/kg)** preserves lean mass and improves satiety. Resistance-trained / leaner
  people need the higher end. Sources: ISSN Position Stand on protein and
  exercise; reviews of dietary protein during caloric restriction.
- Resistance training **2–4×/week** during a deficit is what keeps the result
  "toned" rather than "skinny-fat".

## Diet breaks / intermittent restriction — the science behind grind/chill

- **MATADOR study (Byrne et al., Int. J. Obesity, 2017):** men with obesity who
  alternated **2 weeks of deficit with 2 weeks at maintenance** (intermittent ER)
  lost **more fat, lost less fat-free mass, had less reduction in resting energy
  expenditure (less metabolic adaptation), and kept more weight off at 6 months**
  than an equal "dose" of continuous deficit. This validates the grind/chill and
  maintenance-break approach: planned breaks blunt the body's compensatory
  slowdown and improve long-term results.

## Sleep & stress

- Sleep restriction **raises ghrelin and evening cortisol and lowers leptin**,
  increasing hunger (especially for fat/carb-dense snacks) and undermining diet
  adherence. Poor/short sleep directly reduces dietary compliance. Aim for
  **7.5+ hours**. This matters most for users under high stress / high family
  load with unstable sleep.

## Adherence & diet type

- Meta-analyses show that when **calories and protein are equated**, there is no
  meaningful difference in fat loss between low-carb vs low-fat, or
  time-restricted vs not. **Adherence has a near-linear effect on weight loss.**
  → Pick the *least restrictive* approach the individual can actually sustain;
  switching styles for variety is fine as long as the deficit holds.

## Binge / emotional eating

- The body is wired to self-soothe through eating; chronic restriction + shame
  drives the restrict→binge cycle. Breaking it relies on regular meals, dropping
  rigid "forbidden food" rules, neutral self-talk, hydration, sleep, and
  professional support when needed (see `protocols.md`).
