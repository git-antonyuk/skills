# Protocols

## Grind vs Chill (two diet modes)

- **Grind:** the active deficit. Run it until diet-fatigue signals appear —
  worsening sleep, intrusive food thoughts, physical/mental sluggishness, dropping
  adherence.
- **Chill:** more food, at or near maintenance. Loss slows or pauses, but energy,
  sleep and sanity recover. Ideal for weekends, holidays, high-stress weeks.

Alternate them to avoid burnout and bingeing. When grind starts hurting sleep or
adherence, switch to chill before a blow-up, not after.

## Diet block duration

- Keep a continuous deficit block to **4–12 weeks**.
- Under 4 weeks rarely produces stable results; beyond ~12 weeks fatigue
  accumulates and the risk of falling off climbs.

## Maintenance breaks (the most-skipped step)

- After a full cut, take a deliberate **maintenance phase of ≥8 weeks** at the new
  weight before cutting again — for physiological and psychological recovery.
- The biggest failure mode is having **no post-diet plan**: people hit the goal,
  return to old habits, and regain. Maintenance is a phase you *program*, not a
  default you fall back into. (Backed by MATADOR — see `science.md`.)

## Choosing the diet style

- Adherence beats diet type. Pick whatever restriction feels *least* restrictive
  to this person: calorie/portion control, time-restricted eating, or cutting a
  macro — they're equivalent when calories and protein match.
- Switching styles for variety is fine, as long as the deficit holds.

## Portion control without weighing (Dr. Mike's hand method)

When the user doesn't want to count calories, scale portions to their hand:

- **Protein:** ~1 palm per meal · **Veg:** ~1 fist · **Carbs:** ~1 cupped hand ·
  **Fats:** ~1 thumb. Hand size roughly tracks body size, so it self-scales.

Default meal structure: **~4 meals/day** for stable energy, training quality, and
muscle retention; eat within ~30–60 min after training; don't train right after a
big meal.

## Hunger & diet-fatigue management

- Prioritize protein and fiber; high-volume, low-calorie foods (veg, lean protein)
  for fullness.
- Hydrate — thirst is often misread as hunger.
- Protect sleep (7.5+ h); fatigue erodes impulse control, worst in the evening.
- If fatigue is high: switch to chill, don't white-knuckle.

## Binge-eating prevention (6 levers)

1. **Eat regularly** (every 3–4 h); don't arrive at a meal ravenous. Include
   protein + fiber and foods you actually enjoy.
2. **Watch self-talk** — drop shame/guilt over "bad" food; it worsens the cycle.
3. **No rigid bans** — forbidden foods become more tempting and trigger relapse.
4. **Drink water** regularly.
5. **Sleep 7.5+ h** — poor sleep scrambles hunger/fullness signals.
6. **Get help** when needed — a dietitian for the plan, a therapist for the
   emotional drivers. Recommend this for genuine binge-eating patterns.

## Red flags → recommend a professional

- Intake driven below the calorie floor, loss faster than ~1%/week sustained,
  recurrent binge/restrict cycling, or food-related distress. Coach, not doctor —
  escalate.
